Why Layer 2 Matters

Three reasons developers and users are moving to rollups like Arbitrum.

⚡

Lower Fees

By batching thousands of transactions off-chain and posting a single proof to Ethereum, rollups cut gas costs by up to 90% compared to mainnet.

🔒

Ethereum Security

Arbitrum inherits Ethereum's battle-tested security. Your transactions are backed by the same network that secures billions in value.

🚀

Faster Confirmations

Transactions confirm in seconds instead of minutes. That speed makes DeFi, gaming, and NFT apps feel as responsive as traditional web apps.

Understanding Arbitrum

A quick look at why Layer 2 exists and how Arbitrum fits in.

1 Why Ethereum Needed L2

As Ethereum grew, every transaction competed for limited block space. Simple swaps could cost $20–$50 in gas during peak demand. Layer 2 was built to handle the volume without sacrificing decentralization.

2 What Arbitrum Is

Arbitrum is an Optimistic Rollup — it executes transactions on its own chain, then bundles them into a compressed batch and posts the result back to Ethereum mainnet for final settlement.

3 Real-World Benefit

GMX, a popular decentralized exchange, runs on Arbitrum. Traders swap tokens for pennies instead of dollars in fees, while still settling on Ethereum — the best of both worlds.
